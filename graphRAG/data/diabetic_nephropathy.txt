FILENAME: diabetic_nephropathy.txt
SUBJECT: Chronic Kidney Disease (CKD) in Diabetes
TYPE: Microvascular Complication

1. PATHOPHYSIOLOGY
-----------------------------------------------------------------------------
* **Hyperfiltration:** High blood sugar increases the pressure inside the kidney's filters (Glomeruli).
* **Damage:** Over time, this high pressure damages the filtration barrier.
* **Leakage:** Proteins (Albumin) that should stay in the blood start leaking into the urine.

2. STAGES OF DAMAGE
-----------------------------------------------------------------------------
1.  **Microalbuminuria:** Small amounts of albumin in urine (30â€“300 mg/day). Reversible.
2.  **Macroalbuminuria:** Large amounts of albumin (>300 mg/day).
3.  **Declining GFR:** The Glomerular Filtration Rate drops; waste builds up in blood.
4.  **ESRD:** End-Stage Renal Disease requiring dialysis or transplant.

3. MANAGEMENT
-----------------------------------------------------------------------------
* **BP Control:** Strict blood pressure control (<130/80).
* **ACE Inhibitors / ARBs:** (e.g., Lisinopril, Losartan). These drugs dilate the efferent arteriole, lowering pressure *inside* the kidney.
* **SGLT2 Inhibitors:** Proven to slow the progression of CKD.
