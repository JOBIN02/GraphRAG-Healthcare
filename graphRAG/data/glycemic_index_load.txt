FILENAME: glycemic_index_load.txt
SUBJECT: Nutritional Metrics for Carbohydrate Management

1. GLYCEMIC INDEX (GI) - THE "SPEED"
-----------------------------------------------------------------------------
* **Definition:** Measures how *fast* a food raises blood sugar compared to pure glucose (Scale 0-100).
* **Low GI (<55):** Beans, fibrous veggies, nuts.
* **High GI (>70):** White bread, watermelon, instant oats.
* **Flaw:** Doesn't account for portion size. (Watermelon has high GI, but low carb density).

2. GLYCEMIC LOAD (GL) - THE "IMPACT"
-----------------------------------------------------------------------------
* **Definition:** (GI x Grams of Carbs) / 100. This measures the actual impact of a typical serving size.
* **The Better Metric:** GL is more useful for real-world eating.

3. COMPARISON EXAMPLE
-----------------------------------------------------------------------------
* **Watermelon:**
    * GI = 72 (High - absorbs fast).
    * Carbs per serving = 6g.
    * GL = 4 (Low impact). *Safe in moderation.*
* **Bagel:**
    * GI = 72 (High).
    * Carbs per serving = 50g.
    * GL = 36 (Massive impact). *Avoid.*
