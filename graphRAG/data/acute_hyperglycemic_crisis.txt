FILENAME: acute_hyperglycemic_crisis.txt
SUBJECT: DKA vs. HHS (Emergency Conditions)

1. DIABETIC KETOACIDOSIS (DKA)
-----------------------------------------------------------------------------
* **Patient:** Mostly Type 1 (rarely Type 2).
* **Mechanism:** Absolute lack of insulin. Body burns fat for fuel -> Ketones produced -> Blood becomes acidic.
* **Onset:** Rapid (<24 hours).
* **Signs:** Fruity breath, Kussmaul breathing (deep/fast), High Ketones, pH <7.3.

2. HYPEROSMOLAR HYPERGLYCEMIC STATE (HHS)
-----------------------------------------------------------------------------
* **Patient:** Type 2 (usually elderly).
* **Mechanism:** Severe dehydration and massive hyperglycemia (>600 mg/dL). There is just enough insulin to stop ketones, but not enough to lower sugar.
* **Onset:** Gradual (days to weeks).
* **Signs:** Profound dehydration, confusion/coma, NO Ketones.

3. TREATMENT (HOSPITAL)
-----------------------------------------------------------------------------
* **Fluids:** Aggressive IV saline rehydration (Priority #1).
* **Electrolytes:** Potassium replacement.
* **Insulin:** IV insulin infusion (drip).
