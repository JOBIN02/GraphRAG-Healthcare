FILENAME: insulin_therapy_types.txt
SUBJECT: Pharmacokinetics of Insulin Preparations
USAGE: Basal-Bolus Regimens

1. RAPID-ACTING (BOLUS)
-----------------------------------------------------------------------------
* **Examples:** Lispro (Humalog), Aspart (Novolog), Glulisine (Apidra).
* **Role:** Covers the glucose spike from meals.
* **Onset:** 15 minutes.
* **Peak:** 1 hour.
* **Duration:** 2–4 hours.
* **Timing:** Taken immediately before eating.

2. SHORT-ACTING (REGULAR)
-----------------------------------------------------------------------------
* **Examples:** Humulin R, Novolin R.
* **Role:** Older generation meal coverage (often used in IV drips for DKA).
* **Onset:** 30 mins.
* **Peak:** 2–3 hours.
* **Duration:** 3–6 hours.

3. INTERMEDIATE-ACTING (NPH)
-----------------------------------------------------------------------------
* **Examples:** Humulin N, Novolin N.
* **Appearance:** Cloudy.
* **Role:** Covers basal needs for half the day (often twice daily).
* **Peak:** 4–12 hours (High risk of nocturnal hypoglycemia).

4. LONG-ACTING (BASAL)
-----------------------------------------------------------------------------
* **Examples:** Glargine (Lantus), Detemir (Levemir).
* **Role:** Keeps blood sugar stable between meals and overnight.
* **Onset:** 2–4 hours.
* **Peak:** None (Peakless/Flat profile).
* **Duration:** 24 hours.

5. ULTRA-LONG ACTING
-----------------------------------------------------------------------------
* **Examples:** Degludec (Tresiba), Glargine U-300 (Toujeo).
* **Duration:** >42 hours.
* **Benefit:** Allows flexibility in injection timing.
